Bold vs italic

When to use bold (and when italic is better)

Bold is the workhorse: it's the most legible styled form on both desktop and mobile, which is exactly why it's the most-searched LinkedIn formatting job. Use it for a hook line, a single key phrase, or a section header in a long post.

Italic reads as a softer aside — good for a caveat, a quote, or the opening line of your About section, less so for a hard hook. Italic demand is small, so it lives here as an option rather than its own page: switch to the Cursive or Fancy tab above for italic and script styles.

Is it safe?

Will bold text hurt your reach or searchability?

Not if you do it right. Bold Unicode won't get your account flagged, but two rules keep you safe. First, never bold the keywords you want to be found for — LinkedIn search indexes the underlying characters, so a bold job title can make you harder to find. Second, never bold an entire post — keep your core message in plain text so it stays readable and accessible to screen readers.
