# Bubble Fonts — JTBD & Opportunity Research

**Page:** https://www.ultratextgen.com/category/bubble-fonts/
**Date:** 2026-06-28
**Inputs:** Semrush "bubble letter" broad-match keyword export (6,519 keywords / 517,970 mo US volume), Google Search Console 3‑month export for the bubble page, Semrush keyword overview, plus forum/community research (Reddit, Quora, bullet‑journal & hand‑lettering communities, Discord help).

---

## TL;DR

The bubble page is a clone of the homepage generator. It serves **one** job — *style my digital text with Unicode circled letters to copy‑paste into a bio/caption.* But the keyword and forum evidence shows that **~72% of all "bubble letter" demand is a different job entirely**: *help me MAKE a physical/visual bubble letter* — draw it, trace it, print it, color it, for a poster, sign, card, bullet journal, or classroom. The page captures **none** of that, which is why it earns ~8 clicks / 373 impressions per quarter despite sitting in a ~518K/mo category.

There are two ways to win, and they compound:
1. **Capture the dominant "make a bubble letter" job** the site currently ignores (per‑letter pages, printable/coloring/stencil output, a name→big‑letter canvas).
2. **Deepen the existing "express myself digitally" job** — which is where the user's instinct ("a lot people *can't* do to express") is correct: today you get a tiny inline string and nothing else.

## The evidence

### Search Console — the page barely converts in its own category
- Bubble page, last 3 months: **8 clicks, 373 impressions, 2.14% CTR, avg position 6.42.**
- The queries it *does* surface for are all the Unicode‑generator framing: `bubble font text`, `bubbly characters`, `text font bubble`, `blocky bubble font`, `🅗🅔🅛🅛🅞`. Tiny volumes.
- It surfaces for **none** of the high‑volume head terms below. The content simply doesn't match what most searchers want.

### Keyword demand — the category is enormous and mostly NOT "generator" intent
Total mapped demand: **517,970 searches/mo** across 6,519 keywords. Bucketed by the job the wording implies:

| Signal in the query | Monthly volume | What the searcher actually wants |
|---|---:|---|
| **Per‑letter** (`bubble letter a`, `a bubble letter`, `letter a in bubble letters`, `bubble writing letter a`) | **~373,410** | See / draw / print **one specific letter** |
| **Alphabet (full set)** (`bubble letter alphabet`) | ~22,650 | The whole A–Z as reference/printable |
| **Draw / how‑to / write / make** | ~20,560 | Learn to **hand‑draw** bubble letters |
| **Numbers** | ~15,240 | Bubble **digits** (addresses, ages, jersey #s) |
| **Printable / template / stencil / trace / coloring** | ~10,440 | A **downloadable/printable** asset |
| **Graffiti / street** | ~6,870 | The puffy "balloon graffiti" look |
| **Cute / aesthetic / cool** | ~3,010 | Vibe/identity styling |
| **Names** | ~1,840 | Their **name** in bubble letters |
| Font / generator / copy / paste (the job the page serves) | ~34,580 | Unicode copy‑paste tool |

Head terms: `bubble letter alphabet` (14,800), `bubble letter font` (14,800), `bubble letter` (12,100), `bubble letter a` (12,100), `bubble letter generator` (5,400), `bubble letter necklace` (4,400, commercial), `printable bubble letter` (1,000), `bubble letter graffiti` (1,300).

**Read:** the copy‑paste generator job (~34.6K) is real but is a *minority slice*. The per‑letter + draw + printable jobs (~400K+ combined) dwarf it, and the page addresses them with zero dedicated content.

### Forums & competitors — confirm the "make it" job and show who's eating it
- **How‑to‑draw is a massive content vertical**: lettering‑daily, calligraphy‑skills, easydrawingguides ("how to draw Happy Birthday in bubble letters"), freebiefindingmom. People want pencil‑and‑paper steps, not a Unicode string.
- **Printable / coloring / stencil generators already exist and rank**: colorbliss (bubble‑letter *coloring page* generator), curvedtext.io ("3D bubble letter generator"), freemonogrammaker, homemade‑gifts‑made‑easy ("free printable bubble letters"), bubble‑letters.net ("Happy Birthday bubble letters"). These convert a name/word into a **printable outline you can trace or color** — the exact thing UltraTextGen can't do.
- **Bullet‑journal & hand‑lettering communities** treat bubble headers as an *aesthetic identity* craft ("change spacing/width/colors for variations," "start in pencil," "use the dot grid as a guide"). This is an emotional/identity job, not a utility job.
- **Discord/IG/TikTok communities** use bubble *usernames* and bio dividers for identity — but repeatedly hit two walls: (a) characters **show as squares / get stripped** in some fields, and (b) styled Unicode **breaks screen readers**. Both are unaddressed trust gaps.

## Jobs To Be Done (forum‑grounded)

**Functional / "make it" jobs (today: unserved)**
1. *When I'm making a poster/sign/birthday card for an event, I want a name or word rendered in big bubble letters I can **print, trace, or color**, so I can decorate without design skills* — and feel crafty and proud of the result.
2. *When I'm decorating my bullet journal or class notes, I want **easy bubble‑letter headers I can copy by hand**, so my pages look aesthetic and personal.*
3. *When I'm a kid / parent / teacher, I want **printable bubble alphabet & tracing sheets**, so I can practice or teach letter formation.*
4. *When I only need **one letter or a monogram** (initial, single‑letter logo, address number), I want to grab just that letter big and clean.*

**Social / identity jobs (today: shallowly served)**
5. *When I want my profile to feel like "me," I want a **bubble username/bio that survives paste**, so I express identity and get noticed.*
6. *When I'm hyping a friend in comments/DMs, I want **playful bubble text** so my reaction pops out of the feed* — the "engage socially with others" angle.
7. *When I post to a Story/status, I want a **shareable bubble graphic**, not just inline text, so it works where styled fonts get stripped.*

## What people *can't* express today (the gaps the user sensed)

1. **No single big letter / name.** The dominant intent (373K/mo of per‑letter searches) gets a tiny inline string, not a large, traceable, downloadable letter.
2. **No physical output.** Nothing to print, download, color, or trace. The entire "make it" job is off the table.
3. **Only one truly "bubbly" style.** Only *Ultra Bubble Filled* (🅐) reads as a real inflated letter. Most "bubble" styles are circled/parenthesised (ⓐ, ⒜) — they don't match the **balloon‑graffiti** mental model most searchers picture.
4. **Numbers & scripts are thin.** ~15K/mo want bubble *numbers*; non‑Latin scripts and many symbols pass through unchanged.
5. **No shareable image.** Can't turn output into a PNG for Stories, where styled fonts often get stripped to squares.
6. **No social/collaborative expression.** No couple/duo/group name combos, no "react in bubble," no name‑tag/monogram mode — bubble text is purely a solo copy‑paste act.
7. **Trust gaps unaddressed.** "Why does it show as squares on X?" and "is this accessible?" are recurring forum questions with no on‑page answer or fallback.

## Opportunities (prioritized)

### Tier 1 — Capture the dominant, unserved "make a bubble letter" job
*High volume, low on‑site competition, fits the zero‑framework / static constraint via client‑side SVG + print CSS.*

- **A1 — Per‑letter pages / "Bubble Letter A–Z" hub.** One page per letter (`/category/bubble-fonts/letter-a/` etc.). Each shows the letter **large**, all Unicode bubble variants, **plus** a clean SVG outline to print/trace/color and 3–4 how‑to‑draw steps. Directly targets the ~373K/mo per‑letter intent the site currently forfeits. *(Numbers 0–9 as a sibling set, ~15K/mo.)*
- **A2 — Name → printable bubble‑letter sheet.** Type a name/word, get a big SVG outline you can **download / print / color**. This is the single biggest competitor feature gap (colorbliss, freemonogrammaker, curvedtext all have it; UTG doesn't). Pure client‑side SVG + a print stylesheet — no framework, no backend.
- **A3 — Bubble alphabet + numbers reference page.** Targets `bubble letter alphabet` (14,800) and `printable bubble letter` cluster; natural internal‑link hub to A1.

### Tier 2 — Deepen the digital / social expression job
- **B1 — "Copy as image" (canvas → PNG).** Render the bubble text/letter to a downloadable image for Stories/statuses where Unicode gets stripped. Bridges digital→shareable (JTBD #7).
- **B2 — Identity & social modes.** Username styler with bubble+divider presets for Discord/IG; couple/duo/group‑name combos; monogram/initials mode. Turns a solo tool into a "make something with/for others" tool (JTBD #5, #6).
- **B3 — More authentically *puffy* styles + decorations** (balloon/graffiti‑leaning) so output matches the mental model; lean into the strongest real style (Filled).
- **B4 — Use‑case landing pages** under `/usecase/`: bubble letters for **birthday**, **posters/signs**, **bullet journal**, **classroom**. Each maps to a forum‑proven job and a keyword cluster (`happy birthday bubble letters`, etc.).

### Tier 3 — Trust & quality (cheap differentiators)
- **C1 — Accessibility note + plain‑text companion.** Explain the screen‑reader caveat and offer a plain‑text fallback line. Responsible, and unique vs. competitors.
- **C2 — Platform compatibility hints.** Flag which styles reliably survive IG/TikTok/Discord/Reddit, answering the recurring "shows as squares" frustration.

---

## Recommended first move

Ship **A1 + A3 together** (per‑letter pages + alphabet hub) as the highest‑leverage, on‑brand step: it's static HTML + client‑side SVG (no framework, no backend), it captures the category's center of gravity, and it creates the internal‑link surface that makes **A2** (printable name sheets) the natural follow‑up. Layer **B1/B2** to turn the page from a copy‑paste utility into a place people *make and share* bubble expression — individually and with others.
