**Why it triggers AdSense:** The site has real utility and now has basic legal/trust pages, but the average quality is still dragged down by a large volume of static utility pages, repeated FAQ copy across platform/use-case pages, translation-only localized mirrors, and guide pages with aggressive internal-link repetition.

5. **Internal linking on guides is well above editorial norms.** `/guide/the-rhetoric-of-fonts/index.html` averages **4.07 internal links per 100 words**, and `/guide/stop-the-scroll-with-font-variation/index.html` links `/category/bold-fonts/` **22 times** (`/guide/the-rhetoric-of-fonts/index.html:79-93`, `/guide/the-rhetoric-of-fonts/index.html:385-425`, `/guide/stop-the-scroll-with-font-variation/index.html:173-216`, `/guide/stop-the-scroll-with-font-variation/index.html:618-725`).

## Bottom Line

The repo does **not** look like a scam or empty shell anymore; it now has legal pages, editorial guides, and some genuinely original content. But from an AdSense reviewer’s perspective, the site still reads like a **large static utility network with too many thin or template-adjacent pages per truly distinctive page**. The strongest repo-backed reasons are:

- repeated FAQ copy across multiple route types,
- translation-only localized mirrors,
- very heavy guide-to-tool internal linking,
- and a low valuable-pages-to-total-pages ratio.
