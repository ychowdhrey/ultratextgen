About Roblox Filters

About the Roblox Content Filter

Roblox uses an automatic content filter that may block some Unicode characters in display names and bios. Single-character symbols like ★ ♡ ✦ ❀ ⋆ are the safest. Multi-character combos and obscure Unicode blocks are more likely to be blocked or removed. If a symbol doesn't save in your display name, try a simpler single-character alternative from the Roblox-Friendly Symbols section above.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What symbols work in Roblox display names?
Simple Unicode symbols like ★ ☆ ♡ ♥ ✦ ✧ ❀ ✿ ⋆ ⊹ reliably work in Roblox display names. More complex characters or combining marks may be blocked by Roblox's content filter. Test any symbol by saving your display name — if it saves successfully, the symbol is allowed.
Can I use emoji in my Roblox display name?
Emoji support in Roblox display names is inconsistent and may vary by platform and game. Simple emoji like 🎀 🍓 may work, but emoji are more likely to be filtered than plain Unicode symbols. Stick to text symbols for the most reliable results.
What is the character limit for Roblox display names?
Roblox display names can be 3–20 characters long. Unicode symbols count as one character each, so you have room for 3–6 symbols plus text in a typical display name.
Why did my symbol get removed from my Roblox bio?
Roblox's automatic content filter may remove symbols it doesn't recognize or that match blocked patterns. If your symbol was removed, try a simpler character from the Roblox-Friendly Symbols section. Single-character decorations like ★ and ♡ are the most reliable.
How do I put symbols in my Roblox bio?
Copy any symbol from this page, then go to your Roblox profile settings and paste it into the "About" field. Click save. If the symbol doesn't appear after saving, it was filtered and you'll need to try a different character.
