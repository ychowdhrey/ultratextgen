FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is kawaii?
Kawaii (かわいい) is a Japanese word meaning "cute" or "adorable." As an aesthetic, it encompasses pastel colors, soft imagery, cute animals, and charming symbols. The kawaii aesthetic is popular on social media worldwide, especially on Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest.
What symbols are used in kawaii bios?
Common kawaii bio symbols include ♡ (heart), ✿ (flower), ⋆ (star), ⊹ (cross), ꒰꒱ (brackets), ❀ (florette), ෆ (heart variant), and ୨୧ (wave). These are combined into soft, decorative patterns for bios and usernames.
What are kaomoji and are they the same as kawaii symbols?
Kaomoji are Japanese text emoticons like (˶˃ ᵕ ˂˶) and ʕ•ᴥ•ʔ. They're a subset of kawaii culture. Kawaii symbols also include individual Unicode characters and decorative marks. See our Text Faces & Kaomoji library for a full collection of kaomoji.
Do kawaii symbols work on Discord?
Yes. Discord supports Unicode text and emoji, so all kawaii symbols work in bios, usernames, and messages. Some combining characters may render slightly differently across operating systems.
Can I use these in usernames?
Many platforms allow Unicode characters in display names. For Instagram, TikTok, and Discord display names, most kawaii symbols work. Platform username fields (used for login) are often more restricted — check each platform's specific rules.
