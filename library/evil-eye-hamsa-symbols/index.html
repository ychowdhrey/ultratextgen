Evil Eye & Hamsa Symbols

The nazar amulet 🧿, the hamsa hand 🪬, and the eye and charm symbols that ward off bad luck. Click any symbol to copy it instantly.

The evil eye symbol — known as the nazar in Turkish, Greek, and Middle Eastern traditions — is a blue-and-white eye charm believed to deflect envy and misfortune. Its companion, the hamsa (the Hand of Fatima or Hand of Miriam), carries the same protective meaning across Jewish, Muslim, and Mediterranean cultures. This page collects the nazar amulet emoji, the hamsa emoji, watchful eye characters, and the blue bead palette people use in Instagram bios, jewelry shop listings, and good-luck captions. Note that the hamsa 🪬 was added in Unicode 14, so it may show as a blank box on older devices.
