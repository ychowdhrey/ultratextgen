Understanding Strikethrough Text

A strikethrough text generator is an online tool that converts your plain text into crossed-out text by applying special Unicode combining characters. You simply type or paste your words, and the generator outputs formatted text with a visible horizontal line running through each character ‚Äî ready to copy and paste into emails, social posts, documents, notes, or any text field that supports Unicode.

UltraTextGen lets you generate and copy strikethrough text instantly without memorizing any symbols or codes.
The generator works by inserting a Unicode combining character (such as U+0336, the combining long stroke overlay) after every letter in your input. This invisible modifier instructs the rendering engine to draw a horizontal line through the preceding character.

Because the effect is achieved at the character level rather than through platform-specific formatting, the result is portable ‚Äî it displays consistently across most browsers, apps, and devices without requiring any special software.

Why & When to Use Strikethrough

Strikethrough text adds a visual layer of meaning that plain words alone cannot convey. It lets you show corrections while keeping the original visible, inject irony or humour into social posts, mark completed items in a to-do list, or contrast an old idea with a new one in marketing copy.

By preserving the "deleted" text rather than hiding it, you invite the reader into the editing process ‚Äî making your content feel more transparent, relatable, and engaging.
Strikethrough text is surprisingly versatile. In storytelling, it can reveal a character's inner thoughts or show last-minute revisions. In to-do lists and project updates, it satisfyingly marks completed tasks. In marketing, crossing out an old price next to a new one is a proven attention-grabber.

On social media, it powers ironic humour ‚Äî saying something while "unsaying" it. Educators use it in quizzes to illustrate wrong answers, and designers embed it in infographics to visualise before-and-after comparisons.

Best Practices & Tips
