Types of gothic lettering

"Gothic" covers a family of medieval blackletter scripts. The main ones:

Textura

The earliest, tightest form — narrow, uniform vertical strokes. The "spiky" cathedral look.

Fraktur

German blackletter with "broken" curves. The classic 𝔊𝔬𝔱𝔥𝔦𝔠 alphabet on this page.

Schwabacher

Rounder, bolder cousin of Fraktur — heavy and decorative.

Old English

The chunky tattoo / jersey / newspaper-masthead style. Old English generator →

Gothic text refers to fonts inspired by medieval Blackletter scripts. These fonts feature sharp angles, intricate lines, and decorative flourishes that create a dramatic, vintage feel. It is popular for horror themes, fantasy edits, and edgy social media aesthetics.
