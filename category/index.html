Choosing the Right Font

Bold or Gothic fonts signal authority and intensity. They create visual weight that makes statements feel more decisive.

Bold is the safer choice for professional contexts — LinkedIn, emails, headlines. Gothic goes further, adding drama and edge that works better for gaming, music, and alternative aesthetics.
Bold for headlines and key phrases. Italic for subtle in-line emphasis. Avoid overly playful fonts (Bubble, Upside Down) for a professional tone.

LinkedIn doesn't support native bold in posts, so Unicode bold characters are the only way to make text visually prominent in the feed.
Strikethrough text is the most common way to signal irony or sarcasm in plain text. It creates a contrast between what is said and what is meant — the reader sees both layers.

Example: "This is t̶o̶t̶a̶l̶l̶y̶ fine." — the crossed-out word adds a tone that words alone can't convey.
Bold and Bubble fonts stand out visually and interrupt scrolling. Bold commands authority; Bubble adds playful charm.

For maximum impact, combine a bold style with a word wrapper — it creates a visual container that's hard to scroll past.
Most modern platforms support Unicode, including Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Facebook, X, Discord, WhatsApp, and Telegram.

Because styled characters are real Unicode — not formatting codes — they travel with your text wherever you paste. If a character shows as a box on an older device, try a different style. Core styles like Bold, Italic, and Bubble have the widest support.
