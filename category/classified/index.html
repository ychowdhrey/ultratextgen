General Redacted & Classified Font Questions

A redacted font is a text style that mimics blacked-out or censored content, like what you see in declassified government documents. It uses solid block characters such as █, ▓, and ■ to cover or replace readable text, creating the look of classified information.

Example
Normal text: The agent arrived at 9 PM
Redacted text: The ██████ arrived at █ ██

A classified font replicates the look of government or military redaction — think top-secret stamps, blacked-out lines, and confidential document aesthetics. It is commonly used in memes, social media posts, creative writing, and roleplaying scenarios like SCP entries.

They refer to the same visual concept — text that looks blacked out or censored — but the terms are used in slightly different contexts.

Redacted font usually refers to a stylized text effect where characters are replaced with block symbols like █ or ■. It is the more popular term online and in meme culture.

Redaction font often refers to an actual installable typeface (such as the "Redacted" Google Font) designed for wireframing or prototyping, where the letterforms are intentionally unreadable.

A censored font is any text style that visually blocks, hides, or obscures readable content. This includes blackout bars (████), pixelated overlays, shade blocks (▒▒▒), and square fills (■■■).

Censored fonts are popular for creating joke spoilers, fake "leaked" documents, and dramatic social media posts. The Classified Text Generator offers multiple censored styles — including Redaction, Blocks, Squares, Shades, and Minimal — so you can pick the look that fits your content.
