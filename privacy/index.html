Privacy Policy

How UltraTextGen handles your data, cookies, analytics, and advertising.

Effective Date: April 25, 2026
Last Updated: April 25, 2026

UltraTextGen ("we", "us", or "our") operates the website https://ultratextgen.com (the "Site"). This Privacy Policy explains what information is collected when you visit the Site, how it is used, who we share it with, and the choices you have, including for advertising and analytics.

UltraTextGen is a free, browser-based Unicode text generator. The text you type into the tool is processed locally in your browser and is not sent to or stored on our servers. We do not require accounts, we do not ask for your name, email, address, or phone number to use the tool, and we do not sell your personal information.

By using the Site, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

Information We Collect

We collect a limited amount of information to operate, secure, improve, and monetize the Site through advertising. The categories of information involved are:

1. Information you provide directly.
The Site does not require registration. The only information you actively provide is the text you type into the generator (which stays in your browser) and any message you choose to send us by email.

2. Information collected automatically.
When you visit the Site, our analytics and advertising providers (described below) automatically receive standard log and device information, which may include:

  • IP address (often truncated/anonymized by the provider)
  • approximate, non-precise location derived from the IP address (such as country or city)
  • device type, operating system, browser type and version, and screen size
  • referring/exit pages and the pages you view on the Site
  • date and time of visits and the time spent on pages
  • language preference
  • cookie identifiers and similar advertising identifiers

3. Information stored locally on your device.
We use your browser's localStorage to remember UI preferences (for example, dark mode), recently used styles, and recent generator output so the Site is more convenient to use. This data stays on your device, is never transmitted to our servers, and you can clear it at any time from your browser settings.

We do not collect or store the text you generate on our servers.

Cookies and Similar Technologies

A cookie is a small text file that a website saves on your device to remember information about your visit. Similar technologies include web beacons, pixels, local storage, and advertising identifiers. We and our third-party providers use these technologies for the following purposes:

  • Strictly necessary — to make the Site work (for example, remembering your dark-mode preference).
  • Analytics — to understand which pages are visited and how the Site performs (Google Analytics via Google Tag Manager).
  • Advertising — to show ads, measure ad performance, limit how many times you see the same ad, and (where permitted) personalize the ads you see (Google AdSense and its partners).

You can control cookies through your browser settings — most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Blocking cookies may prevent some features of the Site from working as intended and may not stop you from seeing ads, but the ads may be less relevant to you.
